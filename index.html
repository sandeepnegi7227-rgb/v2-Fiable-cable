Our Story

Built in Narela.
Known across India.

What began in 1998 as a wire drawing unit in Narela Industrial Area has grown into one of Delhi's most respected copper wire manufacturers — a fully integrated facility producing everything from bare conductor to finished insulated cable under one roof.

"Copper is a living material. Every micron of its processing defines whether it carries power or fails under load."
Narela has been the heart of Delhi's industrial manufacturing belt for decades, and Fiable Cables has grown with it. Our plant spans over 5 acres, houses 48 drawing lines, and operates continuous casting equipment that converts copper cathode into rod stock in a single, controlled pass. Every product that leaves our gate carries a full material traceability record — from the cathode batch it was born from to the reel number it ships on.

We supply bare wire, stranded conductors, winding wire, power cable, and flexible/control cables to cable manufacturers, motor winders, switchgear makers, EPC contractors, and exporters. Our laboratory team runs metallurgical verification on every production lot, and our QMS has been ISO 9001 certified for over fifteen years.

Manufacturing Process

From cathode
to finished reel.

Copper Cathode Receipt
Grade A copper cathode (LME registered, ≥99.97% Cu) received, sampled, and batch-recorded against supplier certificate.
Continuous Casting & Rolling
Cathode is melted, continuously cast, and hot-rolled into 8mm copper rod. Oxygen-free variants processed under inert atmosphere.
Multi-Stage Wire Drawing
48 drawing lines reduce rod to wire in controlled stages — 8mm to 0.05mm — with inline annealing to restore ductility and conductivity.
Stranding, Insulation & Jacketing
Wires are stranded to specification, then insulated with PVC, XLPE, LSZH, or enamel depending on product type and application.
QC Testing & Dispatch
100% electrical testing, spark testing, dimensional checks, and final certificate issue. Full batch traceability. Dispatch within 48 hours for standard items.
